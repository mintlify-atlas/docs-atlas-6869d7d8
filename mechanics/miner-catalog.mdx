The HashCash ecosystem runs on 32 distinct miner NFTs, each with a fixed hashrate, power draw, and supply cap. Your earnings are directly proportional to your share of the total network hashrate, so understanding which miners deliver the most hCASH per watt — and per hCASH spent — is the foundation of any serious mining strategy.

## How efficiency is calculated

Every miner in the system has two performance metrics derived from its raw stats:

- **Efficiency (TH/W)** — `hashrate ÷ power`. Higher is better. A miner with 1000 TH/s drawing only 1 W has an efficiency of 1000 TH/W; one with 200 TH/s drawing 1000 W scores only 0.2 TH/W.
- **Daily earnings (hCASH)** — `1.25 × (hashrate ÷ 241,850) × 43,200`. This is the baseline at current network hashrate and post-halving emission rate of 1.25 hCASH per block.
- **Payback (days)** — `priceHCASH ÷ dailyEarnings`. How many days of solo mining to break even on purchase price.

Efficiency matters most for facility planning: your facility has a hard power ceiling, and filling it with low-efficiency miners caps your total hashrate. High-efficiency miners let you extract more TH/s from the same power budget.

<Note>
All earnings projections assume a network hashrate of 241,850 TH/s and 1.25 hCASH per block. As more players join and network hashrate grows, your share — and your daily yield — will decrease proportionally.
</Note>

## Secondary market

All miner NFTs can be traded peer-to-peer for hCASH or AVAX. Some miners (notably WindHash Red/Blue and RedDragon Tri-Rig) have dual-currency primary prices; secondary market prices are set freely by sellers. Special-issue and not-for-sale miners are exclusively obtainable on the secondary market, and their scarcity typically commands significant premiums.
